The Braided Pile Rug is a deconstruction and reconstruction of the archetypal Colonial American rug.

Introducing a secondary open layer of braided cord to the rug’s surface gives a richer topography and irregular graphic density to the normally flat and orderly typology. Approx. 180 x 275cm

SMF glassware investigates what defines a “family” of objects. A legible sense of consistency is achieved through economizing the method of production.

The three-piece set is blown in one mold and later cut to volume based on slight demarcations in the glass’ profile. Each size is formally autonomous but together the collection reads as familial owing to their origin: the Single Mold Family.

Concave key responds to an observed behavior. When unlocking a door, a key oftentimes circumvents the job of a door handle. Insert, twist, and push—actions that take place while grasping the small key rather than the more comfortable intended point of contact: the doorknob.

The concave top of this key provides a softer edge and more generous surface to grip while locking and unlocking. Multiples nest on a key ring.

The objective of this cutlery set—fork, knife, spoon—is to communicate with scale. The proportions and prescribed purposes attributed to cutlery today can seem leftover from a dining culture of more formal conventions.

The modest size and voluptuous handles of this cutlery make it more characteristic of today’s table: refined, broadly useful, and easygoing.

A bar of soap typically affords just enough heft to feel significant, but quickly recedes to a sliver. Scale and weight presented a window of opportunity to be disruptive but respectful in relation to soap as a tool.

Large soap prompts a conceptual conversation about the use of a pedestrian yet indispensable object. Approx. 17 x 7 x 4.5cm
